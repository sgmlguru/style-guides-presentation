Not an ordered list

Several misunderstandings here. This is not an unordered list - bullets mean "order is not important, I'm just listing items".

It's also not an ordered list - an ordered list is something describing items in order of preference.

It's a procedure. It looks like an ordered list but it's anything but.

The bullets are a misguided attempt at looking pretty

I'm sure we've all seen this sort of thing

                            <emphasis type="bold italic">emphasised content</emphasis>

Spare part? GUI object? Legal term? Some random important word with pretty formatting?

Schema documentation autogenerated and not up to date. Often, this does not mean anything, at lest not consistently. But witness the legal doc list problem.

So?

We've analysed docs

We've created a DTD that describes them

We've (hopefully) identified the various types of lists

We've (maybe) identified inline semantics

But I'm betting we haven't described how and when to use any of them.

It could be (and often is) that it really is about looking pretty and nothing more.

It could also be that the analysis didn't discover the why.

Or it could be that different writers do the same thing differently.

Or, conversely, that this writer simply did what (he thought) the previous writer did.

We don't know unless you tell us.
